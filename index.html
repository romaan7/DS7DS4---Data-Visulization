Visualizing Syrian Refugees Crysis

Syria has been a country in constant conflict of civil war for the past seven years. With different groups trying to seize control over the country like IS and anti-government groups forming the situation is worsening day by day. Crowded cities have been destroyed and horrific human rights violations are widespread. Basic necessities like food and medical care are sparse. The country is turning uninhabitable with lack of basic amenities. People are looking for ways out of the country.

The U.N. estimates that 6.2 million people are internally displaced [1]. When you also consider refugees, well over half of the country’s pre-war population of 22 million is in need of urgent humanitarian assistance, whether they still remain in the country or have escaped across the borders. Let us have a look over statics for the past few years and try to understand the situation.

Where do the refegues find asylum.?

The current civil war climate has made the country inhabitable and the people have started to move out of the country to survive for their livelihood. The Force chart visualized below is an appropriate depiction of the data. Each circle in the chart displays the country and the number of refugees that have immigrated. With the current climate in the country, we infer that most of the people have immigrated into continents that are sharing borders. Each new diagram represents the data for the year between 2010 and 2014. When clicked on 'Above 1000' the navigation menu on the top left, the view only highlights the countries with Refugees above 1000.
